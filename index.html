Indian BlackBuck

The Black Buck is an endangered antelope found only in India and Nepal. This graceful and beautiful animal is found roaming in the plains of India except the western coast of India. Nowadays, in India this animal is found in many national parks of India like Guindy National Park, Point Calimere and Vellandu Sanctuaries of Tamil Nadu, Rollapadu of Andhra Pradesh.

Snow Leopard

Snow leopards have evolved to live in some of the harshest conditions on Earth. Their thick white-gray coat spotted with large black rosettes blends in perfectly with Asia’s steep and rocky, high mountains. Because of their incredible natural camouflage, rendering them almost invisible in their surroundings, snow leopards are often referred to as the “ghost of the mountains.”

Red Panda

The red panda is slightly larger than a domestic cat with a bear-like body and thick russet fur. The belly and limbs are black, and there are white markings on the side of the head and above its small eyes. Red pandas are very skillful and acrobatic animals that predominantly stay in trees. Almost 50% of the red panda’s habitat is in the Eastern Himalayas..

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the primary focus of WildTrack?

WildTrack is dedicated to wildlife conservation, aiming to raise awareness and promote initiatives that safeguard the diverse ecosystems and species inhabiting our planet.

How can I contribute to Wildtrack's conservation efforts?

You can contribute by staying informed, sharing our content, participating in conservation projects, and supporting affiliated initiatives. Visit our "Get Involved" section for more details.

What sets WildTrack apart in the realm of wildlife conservation?

WildTrack combines scientific expertise with innovative technologies to address conservation challenges. From drone-based habitat monitoring to bioacoustic research, our approach is cutting-edge and impactful..

Are there opportunities for collaboration or partnerships with WildTrack?

Yes, WildTrack welcomes collaborations with like-minded organizations, researchers, and individuals. Feel free to reach out through our contact section for potential partnerships.

How does WildTrack ensure transparency in its conservation projects?

Transparency is a core value. We provide regular updates on ongoing projects, share outcomes, and engage with our community through newsletters, social media, and detailed project reports.

What role does community engagement play in WildTrack's initiatives?

Community engagement is pivotal. WilTrack believes in fostering local support and empowering communities to be stewards of their environments, ensuring sustainable conservation outcomes.
